Patient education is the process of providing patients with the knowledge and skills they need to take control of their health and well-being. It is a crucial component of healthcare that involves sharing information, resources, and support with individuals to help them understand their conditions, treatment options, and ways to manage their health effectively. The goal of patient education is not only to inform but also to empower patients. By understanding their diagnosis, treatment plans, and preventive care strategies.

Encourage patients to brush their teeth at least twice a day using fluoride toothpaste and a soft-bristled toothbrush, focusing on all surfaces of the teeth and along the gumline. Flossing daily is essential to remove plaque and food particles from between teeth, where a toothbrush cannot reach. Patients should also be advised to limit sugary foods and beverages and to drink plenty of water to maintain oral health. Regular dental visits every six months for professional cleanings and examinations are crucial to detect and address issues like cavities, gum disease, or oral infections early.
